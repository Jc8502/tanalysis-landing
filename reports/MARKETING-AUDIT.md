## Executive Summary

Tanalysis scores 61/100 — a functional but underleveraged marketing presence for a product with genuinely differentiated technology. The platform's core competitive asset — a patent-specialized AI model trained on 80 million records of actual Korean Intellectual Property Office examiner data — is real, specific, and compelling. That asset is partially visible in the site copy but consistently undersold, buried under vague efficiency language rather than foregrounded as a category-defining technical moat.

The biggest strength is the feature comparison table and the quantified proof points: 85% recall rate (4x competitors), 90%+ classification accuracy, 10,000 patents classified in 5 minutes. These numbers exist on the site but are placed too deep in the funnel, too late in the page, and without the aggressive headline treatment they deserve. A first-time visitor cannot immediately answer the question "why is this different from ChatGPT or other patent search tools?" — and for a B2B SaaS targeting professional buyers, that question must be answered in under five seconds.

The biggest gap is conversion architecture. The site's primary CTA "무료로 시작하기" (Start for Free) leads to a manual approval flow — the company confirms accounts within one business day. This is a significant friction point that is not managed well: there is no explicit expectation-setting, no interim nurture step, no email sequence described. The Weekly Report product (reportbymail) is the most strategically interesting offer on the site — it delivers recurring value automatically and addresses a real executive pain point — but it is buried in the navigation and has no prominent placement on the homepage.

The top three actions that would move the needle immediately: (1) Rewrite the homepage H1 around the 85% recall / examiner-level accuracy proof point rather than the generic "업무 효율화" framing; (2) Add an explicit conversion path for the Weekly Report directly on the homepage — it is a lower-friction entry point than the full product trial; (3) Fix the approval-delay friction by adding an immediate value step (sample report, demo video, or onboarding email) that triggers on signup before approval is granted. Implementing all recommendations in this audit is estimated to improve lead volume by 30-60% and conversion-to-trial by 15-25%, representing a meaningful monthly revenue lift for a B2B SaaS at typical Korean enterprise deal sizes.

## Strategic Recommendations (This Month)

### 1. Build a Dedicated Comparison Landing Page
**Rationale:** The site currently has a comparison table (Before/After Tanalysis) but no page addressing the specific alternatives decision-makers are considering: Wips-on, Kipris, Patentpia, or generic LLM tools. The copy "다 같은 AI가 아닙니다" signals awareness of this comparison need but does not fulfill it.
**Implementation:**
- Create /vs-kipris, /vs-wips, /vs-chatgpt pages (or a single /comparison page with tabs)
- Lead with the specific differentiation: patent-specialized model vs. general LLM, full-text search vs. abstract-only, 85% recall vs. industry average
- Include the feature comparison table with named competitors
**Expected Outcome:** Captures high-intent search traffic from "타날리시스 vs XX" queries; shortens sales cycle by answering the most common objection before a sales call

### 2. Create a Segmented Landing Page Per Buyer Persona
**Rationale:** The site serves three distinct buyers with different pain points: patent attorneys (변리사) at law firms, in-house IP/legal teams, and R&D teams. The current homepage speaks to all three simultaneously, which means it speaks to none of them optimally.
**Implementation:**
- /for-patent-firms — focus: speed, accuracy, reducing per-file labor cost
- /for-inhouse-ip — focus: portfolio management, IP risk monitoring, executive reporting
- /for-rd — focus: prior art search, FTO analysis, competitive landscape
- Link from the navigation "분야별 사례" section
**Expected Outcome:** Higher relevance = higher conversion. Each page can have persona-specific CTAs, testimonials, and case studies.

### 3. Build a Post-Signup Onboarding Email Sequence
**Rationale:** The current signup flow ends with a manual approval delay (up to 1 business day). There is no described nurture sequence. B2B SaaS products with complex workflows have significantly lower activation rates without guided onboarding.
**Implementation:**
- Email 1 (immediate): Confirmation + "while you wait, here's a 2-minute explainer" + link to 소개자료
- Email 2 (approval day): Welcome + first task tutorial ("Try your first search in 3 steps")
- Email 3 (Day 3): "Did you know you can analyze 10,000 patents in 5 minutes?" — classification feature
- Email 4 (Day 7): Case study relevant to user's industry
- Email 5 (Day 14): Trial ending / upgrade prompt with ROI framing
**Expected Outcome:** 20-40% improvement in trial-to-paid conversion based on B2B SaaS benchmarks for onboarding sequences

### 4. Develop a Pricing Page with Clear Tier Articulation
**Rationale:** Pricing is currently only visible in the FAQ under "포인트 차감량 안내" — a deeply unintuitive location. There is no dedicated pricing page. Enterprise buyers will not go hunting for pricing in an FAQ.
**Implementation:**
- Create /pricing with three tiers clearly labeled: Free (20pt/day), Business (1,000pt/month), Premium/Enterprise (협의)
- Include a feature comparison matrix per tier
- Add a cost-benefit calculator: "At X searches/month, Business plan pays for itself in Y hours saved"
- Address the most common pricing objection: "Is this worth it vs. doing it manually?"
**Expected Outcome:** Reduces sales cycle length; allows self-serve evaluation; improves trial quality

### 5. Establish a LinkedIn + Professional Community Presence
**Rationale:** The target buyers (변리사, IP managers, R&D leads) are active on LinkedIn and Korean professional communities (특허 관련 카카오 오픈채팅, 변리사 커뮤니티). The site has no visible social media links and no community angle.
**Implementation:**
- Create a company LinkedIn page with weekly posts sharing patent trend insights (leverage the Weekly Report content)
- Share one case study or insight per week derived from real usage patterns (anonymized)
- Consider a 변리사 커뮤니티 내 무료 체험 offer tied to referrals
**Expected Outcome:** Organic brand awareness in a highly word-of-mouth-driven professional community; long-term pipeline development

### 6. Add Schema Markup for SaaS/Software Product
**Rationale:** The site is built on Framer (client-side rendered). It currently lacks any structured data markup. Adding SoftwareApplication schema and FAQPage schema would improve rich snippet eligibility.
**Implementation:**
- Add `SoftwareApplication` schema to homepage and feature pages
- Add `FAQPage` schema to all FAQ pages (already have excellent FAQ content)
- Add `Organization` schema with address, phone, and sameAs links
**Expected Outcome:** Improved Google rich snippet display; potentially significant CTR improvement from search results

The "앞서가는 전문가들은 이미 타날리시스를 도입했습니다" line on the homepage is a social proof tease with no substance behind it on the homepage itself.

**Content Depth:**
The blog is listed in navigation but appears to have no substantial content (not captured in page cache). For a B2B SaaS targeting professionals who make evidence-based purchasing decisions, the absence of educational content is a material gap.

**Brand Voice:**
Consistent across pages — professional, Korean-native, avoids overly formal legal register while maintaining technical credibility. The tagline "변리사가 만들어 더 정확한 AI 특허 분석" (footer) is excellent — it leads with the most important trust signal (built by patent attorneys, not generic AI engineers) and should be elevated to the homepage hero.

Based on page content, the site is targeting: 특허 AI 검색, AI 특허 분류, 특허 분석. Missing from copy but highly relevant: 선행기술조사, 특허 선행조사, 무효자료 조사, FTO 분석, 특허 포트폴리오 분석, 변리사 AI 도구. These are high-intent keywords used by actual buyers.
