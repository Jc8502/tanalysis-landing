## Executive Summary

Tanalysis operates in the Korean B2B patent search and analysis SaaS market — a space that is undergoing rapid transformation as AI capabilities challenge the traditional dominance of government-backed free databases and legacy enterprise platforms. The competitive landscape is two-tiered: on one side sits KIPRIS (the Korean Intellectual Property Office's free public search service) which defines the floor expectation for any Korean patent professional; on the other sit global enterprise powerhouses like Patsnap, Derwent Innovation, and Keywert that compete on breadth of data and brand recognition.

Tanalysis's core competitive position is unique and defensible: it is the only AI model trained on Korean Patent Office (KIPO) rejection and invalidation data. This produces a measurably superior recall rate — 85% versus examiner citations, self-reported as 4x better than competitors — combined with full-text search (not abstract-only, as many competitors do), 30-second search speed across 80 million patents, and a workflow that replaces complex Boolean search strings with natural language sentence input. This is not a marginal feature difference; it is a fundamentally different epistemological approach to patent AI.

The biggest competitive advantage is the examiner-trained model and the component-based analysis workflow (구성 카드), which directly maps to how Korean patent attorneys and IP teams actually work — by decomposing claims into constituent elements. No competitor explicitly replicates this workflow with this level of local specificity. The biggest competitive threat is Keywert (워트인텔리전스), which is a well-funded Korean AI patent search platform with 300 million+ patents, litigation data integration, and active marketing to the same professional audience. A secondary threat is the continued zero-cost availability of KIPRIS, which creates a high price-sensitivity baseline in the market.

The top three strategic recommendations are: (1) aggressively create "Tanalysis vs KIPRIS," "Tanalysis vs Keywert," and "Tanalysis vs Patsnap" comparison landing pages to capture high-intent bottom-of-funnel search traffic; (2) commission and publish a third-party validated benchmark study confirming the 85% recall rate, which would transform an internal claim into an industry-cited proof point; and (3) develop a structured content strategy targeting Korean IP professionals searching for efficiency workflows, claim chart automation, and AI-based patent classification — areas where Tanalysis has real product depth but limited content visibility.
