# Copy Analysis & Suggestions: https://tanalysis.co.kr/
**Date:** 2026-03-07
**Business:** Tanalysis (타날리시스) — Korean B2B SaaS, AI-powered patent search, analysis, and classification
**Target Users:** Patent attorneys (변리사), in-house IP teams, R&D teams, corporate IP strategy managers
**Page Type:** Homepage (primary analysis) + Product/Feature pages (secondary)
**Copy Score:** 54/100

---

## Executive Summary

Tanalysis has a genuinely differentiated product — the only Korean patent AI built on actual patent examination data (심사 데이터), with an 85% recall rate that is 4x better than competitors. The product story is strong. The copy, however, consistently buries the lead. The current homepage headline, "업무 효율화, 정확한 특허 검색·분석부터" (Work efficiency, starting from accurate patent search and analysis), reads like a category description rather than a value proposition. It could belong to any generic SaaS tool. Visitors who land on the page do not immediately understand *why* Tanalysis is uniquely capable or what outcome they will achieve.

The core problem throughout the site is a preference for describing the product over communicating transformation. Copy like "변리사와 AI 전문가가 처음부터 만들어, 정확도와 실무 적합도가 매우 높습니다" (Built by patent attorneys and AI experts from the ground up, with very high accuracy and practical fit) is a factual statement, not a persuasive claim. The page has all the raw ingredients — a compelling 85% recall stat, four real testimonials, a clear before/after comparison table, and a credible founding team — but these assets are not being used at maximum leverage. The 85% stat, for instance, does not appear on the homepage at all; it is buried on the patent search subpage.

The site also suffers from CTA fragmentation. The homepage presents five different calls to action (소개자료 받기, 자세히 보기, 타날리시스팀 문의, 도입 문의, 지금 가입하면 무료) without clear hierarchy. This forces the visitor to make a decision the page should make for them. Priority fixes: (1) rewrite the hero headline and subhead to lead with the 85% recall stat or equivalent transformation outcome, (2) consolidate CTAs around a single primary action per page section, (3) translate feature descriptions into role-specific benefit statements that speak to the professional identity of the target user.

## Score Breakdown

### Clarity: 6/10
The site is understandable once you read multiple sections, but the hero section does not immediately answer "what is this and why should I care." The H1 "업무 효율화" is too broad — efficiency for whom, doing what, compared to what baseline? The feature names (구성 분석, 구성카드, 구성값) use internal jargon that is meaningful to patent attorneys but opaque to R&D managers or IP strategy executives who are also listed as target users. The comparison table (Before/After Tanalysis) is the clearest copy on the entire page and should be elevated significantly.

### Persuasion: 5/10
The site lists features effectively but rarely closes the persuasive loop. "복잡한 검색식은 NO, 문장으로 입력해도 AI가 정확하게 특허 검색" (No complex search strings, input in sentences and AI searches accurately) explains how the product works but does not explain what that means for the user's workday, billable hours, or professional reputation. Testimonials exist and are specific, but they are all from a single subpage (/patent-search) and do not appear on the homepage where they would have the most impact. The classification page's 90% accuracy stat and 10,000 document capacity are powerful proof points that never surface in the main sales narrative.

### Specificity: 7/10
This is the site's strongest dimension. The patent search page delivers real numbers: 85% recall, 4x competitor performance, 30-second search for 2,000 documents, 80 million records. The classification page delivers 10,000 documents, 5 minutes, 90%+ accuracy, 4.3/5 satisfaction. These are excellent specifics. The problem is that these numbers live on inner pages. The homepage, which most visitors see first, has almost no concrete figures. Specificity score on the homepage alone would be 4/10.

### Emotion: 4/10
The weekly report page ("경쟁사가 어디로 가는지 이제 찾아보지 않아도 됩니다" — You no longer have to look up where your competitors are going) and its pain point section ("이미 늦었을 수도 있어요" — You might already be too late) are the emotional high points of the site. These connect to real professional anxiety: the fear of missing a competitor's strategic patent move. This emotional register should be expanded to the homepage hero. The current homepage treats the user's problem as an operational inconvenience (too much repetitive work) when it could be framed as a professional and business risk (missed prior art, invalid patents, IP blindspots).

### Action: 4/10
Five distinct CTAs on the homepage with no clear hierarchy is a conversion antipattern. "소개자료 받기" (Get an introduction document) is a low-commitment, high-friction CTA that delays trial. "지금 가입하면 무료" (Sign up now, it's free) is the correct primary CTA but it is not the most prominent. The navigation CTA "무료로 시작하기" (Start for free) is well-positioned. CTAs lack first-person framing ("지금 무료로 시작하기" vs "저도 무료로 시작할게요") and most do not include a benefit statement or risk reducer.

## Value Proposition Analysis

```
TARGET CUSTOMER:
  Primary: Patent attorneys (변리사) at Korean IP firms who conduct prior art searches,
  invalidity investigations, and FTO analysis daily.
  Secondary: In-house IP team members at large Korean corporations (chaebol, tech companies)
  managing patent portfolios and monitoring competitor IP activity.
  Tertiary: R&D researchers who need to identify whitespace and avoid inadvertent infringement.

PROBLEM:
  Patent search in Korean practice requires building complex Boolean search strings (검색식)
  that are slow to construct, prone to recall gaps, and must be rebuilt for each search element
  (구성) of a claim. Current commercial AI tools (ChatGPT, etc.) hallucinate patent citations
  and raise data security concerns. Classifying large patent sets is a manual, multi-day task.
  Monitoring competitor IP activity requires weekly manual searches that almost never happen.

SOLUTION:
  Tanalysis replaces search string construction with natural-language, claim-element-by-element
  semantic search trained on actual patent examination outcomes. AI classification handles
  bulk documents (up to 10,000) in 5 minutes. Weekly reports automate competitor monitoring.

UNIQUE MECHANISM:
  The AI model is trained on patent examination data from the Korean Intellectual Property
  Office (특허청) and the Intellectual Property Trial and Appeal Board (특허심판원) — rejection
  and invalidity decisions. This is described as the only patent-specialized model in Korea
  trained on this data. This means it "thinks like an examiner" rather than using general
  semantic similarity, which is why recall significantly outperforms general AI tools.

KEY BENEFIT:
  Patent attorneys and IP teams can do in minutes what previously took hours — and find
  prior art that other tools miss, reducing professional risk and increasing throughput.

PROOF:
  - 85% recall rate on examiner-cited documents (vs ~21% for competitors, by own testing)
  - 10,000 documents classified in 5 minutes at 90%+ accuracy
  - 4 real testimonials from named roles (화학기업 연구원, 자율주행 업체 책임 연구원,
    바이오 분야 대기업 변리사)
  - 80 million patent records across 6 jurisdictions (KR, US, JP, EP, CN, PCT)

GAPS IN CURRENT COPY:
  - The unique mechanism (trained on examination decisions) is the single most differentiating
    fact about this product and it does not appear on the homepage.
  - No customer count, firm count, or "patents searched" volume metric on the homepage.
  - The 85% recall stat (the product's strongest proof point) is absent from the homepage.
  - Testimonials are buried on an inner product page, not surfaced on the homepage.
  - The professional risk angle (missed prior art = invalid patent = professional liability)
    is never made explicit.
```

## Section-by-Section Copy Suggestions

### Section 1: Hero / Above the Fold

**Current Copy:**
- H1: 업무 효율화, / 정확한 특허 검색·분석부터
- Sub-labels: AI 특허 검색·분석 / AI 특허 분류
- Body: 찾는 문헌을 잘 찾고, 분석까지 쉬운 타날리시스로 반복 업무 부담을 줄여보세요
- CTA: 소개자료 받기

**Issues:**
- "업무 효율화" is the weakest possible opening — every B2B tool claims efficiency
- Body copy repeats the headline's meaning with no new information
- "소개자료 받기" as the primary CTA sends the visitor away from the conversion path before they have made any commitment; it is a pre-conversion step masquerading as a primary CTA

**Recommended Hero Copy:**
```
H1: 다른 DB가 놓친 선행문헌, 타날리시스는 찾아냅니다.

Subhead: 특허청 심사 데이터로 학습한 유일한 특허 AI.
         검색식 없이, 문장 입력만으로, 재현율 85%.

Body: 변리사가 직접 설계한 AI로 선행기술 조사, 무효자료 수집,
      특허 분류를 하나의 도구에서 끝내세요.

Primary CTA: 지금 무료로 시작하기 →
Secondary CTA: 소개자료 받기 (smaller, less prominent)
```

**Rationale:** The strongest differentiator (examiner data training) and strongest proof point (85% recall) are now in the hero. "소개자료 받기" is demoted to secondary. The body copy names the three specific professional tasks (prior art, invalidity, classification) so every target user self-identifies immediately.

### Section 2: Feature Highlights (쉽고 정확한 검색 / 편리한 구성별 분석 / 분류 자동화)

**Current H3s:**
- 쉽고 정확한 검색으로 *(With easy and accurate search)*
- 편리한 구성별 분석으로 *(With convenient element-by-element analysis)*
- 분류 자동화로 빠르게 *(Quickly with classification automation)*

**Issues:**
- All three headings describe the feature mode ("with X"), not the user outcome
- "쉽고" (easy), "편리한" (convenient), "빠르게" (quickly) are assertions, not evidence
- These three headings are structurally parallel but emotionally inert — they could be from any SaaS product

**Recommended Feature Headlines:**
```
BEFORE: 쉽고 정확한 검색으로
AFTER:  놓치는 문헌 없이 — 검색식 없는 의미 기반 검색

BEFORE: 편리한 구성별 분석으로
AFTER:  구성별 유사도를 한눈에 — 원문 근거까지 자동 제공

BEFORE: 분류 자동화로 빠르게
AFTER:  1만 건, 5분 완성 — 수작업 없는 AI 특허 분류
```

**Rationale:** Each revised heading includes a specific outcome (no missed documents, visual similarity comparison, 10,000 in 5 minutes) and each removes the passive "-로" construction in favor of an active outcome statement. The classification headline uses the page's own statistics, which are strong enough to be the heading.

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Primary Homepage Headline

**BEFORE:**
> 업무 효율화, 정확한 특허 검색·분석부터

**AFTER:**
> 다른 DB가 놓친 선행문헌, 타날리시스는 찾아냅니다. 재현율 85%.

**WHY:** The "before" headline describes an abstract outcome (efficiency) attributed to the product. The "after" headline names the specific failure of competing tools (missed prior art), makes the claim concrete with a number (85% recall), and implies professional risk for the user — all in one sentence. The "after" passes the 5-second test; the "before" does not.

---

### Example 2: Subheadline / Body Lead

**BEFORE:**
> 찾는 문헌을 잘 찾고, 분析까지 쉬운 타날리시스로 반복 업무 부담을 줄여보세요

**AFTER:**
> 특허청 심사 데이터로 학습한 유일한 AI. 검색식 없이, 문장으로 입력하면 30초 안에 2천 건 검색. 변리사와 AI 전문가가 만든 이유가 있습니다.

**WHY:** The "before" version is a gentle invitation ("줄여보세요" — try reducing). It is polite but toothless. The "after" version makes three specific claims (unique training data, no search strings, 30-second/2,000-document search) that require the visitor to either believe the claim and keep reading or dismiss it — both outcomes are better than a forgettable general statement.

---

### Example 3: Primary CTA

**BEFORE:**
> 소개자료 받기

**AFTER:**
> 지금 무료로 시작하기 →

**WHY:** "소개자료 받기" is a passive, pre-conversion CTA that takes the visitor off the main funnel to receive a PDF brochure. The intended next step for most B2B SaaS visitors is a trial, not a brochure. "지금 무료로 시작하기" is immediate ("지금" — now), removes perceived financial risk ("무료" — free), describes the action clearly, and uses directional punctuation ("→") to signal forward movement. For enterprise prospects who need stakeholder buy-in, a secondary "소개자료 받기" text link is appropriate, but it should not be the primary CTA.

---

### Example 4: Feature Description (Classification)

**BEFORE:**
> 대량 문헌을 AI가 스스로 분류하고 인사이트 시각화까지 (프리미엄 기능)

**AFTER:**
> 1만 건의 특허를 5분에 분류. 정확도 90% 이상. AI가 기준을 제안하고, 분류 결과를 13개 그래프로 시각화합니다. 별도 학습 불필요.

**WHY:** The "before" version uses "대량" (large volume) — a vague quantity — and "인사이트 시각화" — a buzzword that does not specify what kind of insight or how many visualizations. The "after" version makes every element concrete: 10,000 documents, 5 minutes, 90%+ accuracy, 13 graphs, no training required. Each element addresses a specific objection a potential buyer would have (How many? How long? How accurate? How complex to set up?).

---

### Example 5: Meta Description

**BEFORE:**
> 타날리시스 AI가 특허를 빠르고 정확하게 찾아 분析해 드립니다. 경쟁사 핵심 기술 분析과 AI 특허 분류까지.

**AFTER:**
> 특허청 심사 데이터 기반 AI. 재현율 85%, 타사 대비 4배. 변리사·IP팀·R&D용 특허 검색·분析·분류. 무료로 시작하세요.

**WHY:** Meta descriptions influence click-through rate from search results. The "before" version has no differentiator, no number, and no specific audience address. The "after" version leads with the unique mechanism (KIPO data), follows with the key proof point (85%, 4x), names all three target audiences, and ends with a CTA. Every phrase in the "after" is load-bearing.

## Implementation Priority

Ranked by estimated conversion and brand impact:

### Priority 1 — Immediate (Homepage Hero Rewrite)
**Impact: Very High | Effort: Low**
Rewrite the H1 to lead with the 85% recall stat or the examiner-data differentiator. This is the single highest-leverage change on the site. Every visitor sees the hero; almost no one reaches the inner stats pages. Current estimated copy contribution to bounce rate is high because the hero fails to communicate differentiation.

### Priority 2 — Immediate (Elevate Testimonials to Homepage)
**Impact: High | Effort: Very Low**
Move the three existing testimonials from /patent-search to the homepage. No new content is needed. This is a layout decision that can be implemented in Framer in under one hour and will meaningfully improve the persuasion score of the homepage.

### Priority 3 — High (CTA Hierarchy Simplification)
**Impact: High | Effort: Low**
Establish one primary CTA per page section. On the homepage, make "무료로 시작하기" the dominant button and demote "소개자료 받기" to a text link. On enterprise/contact sections, use "15분 무료 상담 신청하기." This change requires no new copywriting — only visual hierarchy adjustment.

### Priority 4 — High (Add 85% Recall Stat to Homepage)
**Impact: High | Effort: Low**
The 85% recall rate (대비 4배) is the most credible specific claim the product has and it does not appear on the homepage. Add it to either the hero subhead or a stats bar near the top of the page. Adjacent stats to display together: 85% 재현율 / 8천만 건 / 30초 / 90% 분류 정확도.

### Priority 5 — Medium (Feature Description Rewrites)
**Impact: Medium | Effort: Medium**
Rewrite the three main feature section headings (쉽고 정확한 검색으로 / 편리한 구성별 분析으로 / 분류 자동화로 빠르게) to outcome-based headlines with specific numbers. See Section-by-Section recommendations above.

### Priority 6 — Medium (Meta Description Update)
**Impact: Medium | Effort: Very Low**
Update the homepage meta description to include the 85% stat, unique mechanism, and CTA. This is a 10-minute change with ongoing SEO and CTR benefits.

### Priority 7 — Medium (Differentiator Copy Consolidation)
**Impact: Medium | Effort: Low**
The "변리사와 AI 전문가가 만들었다" (built by patent attorneys and AI experts) claim appears twice in nearly identical form. Consolidate into one clear version that names the specific training data (KIPO/IPTAB examination decisions) and the specific outcome (85% recall). Remove the duplicate.

### Priority 8 — Lower (Weekly Report Page Headline Refinement)
**Impact: Medium | Effort: Very Low**
The weekly report page headline is already strong. Minor refinement to add delivery cadence specificity (Monday morning, weekly). Also add the government subsidy deadline CTA ("최대 3,000만 원 정부 지원, ~3월 31일") more prominently — deadline-based urgency is powerful for enterprise prospects.

### Priority 9 — Lower (Pricing Page Framing)
**Impact: Medium | Effort: Medium**
The points-based pricing is presented as a FAQ/guide rather than as a pricing page. Consider adding a pricing section headline that frames the points system positively ("하루 20건 무료로 시작, 업무량에 맞춰 확장") and addresses the most common objection (포인트 이월 불가 — points do not roll over). Proactively address this in the copy rather than burying it in an FAQ.

### Priority 10 — Strategic (Customer Count / Firm Count Social Proof)
**Impact: High when available | Effort: Medium**
The site currently has no aggregate social proof metric (no "X firms use Tanalysis" or "Y patents searched"). When this data becomes available, add it to the homepage social proof bar. Even modest numbers (e.g., "50개 기업·법인 도입") significantly increase conversion for first-time visitors who are evaluating risk.
