# Brand Voice Guidelines
## Tanalysis (타날리시스)
### Analysis Date: 2026-03-07

---

## Voice Summary

Tanalysis communicates as a credible expert built by practitioners for practitioners — a voice of quiet, evidence-backed authority that avoids the hollow hype common in AI SaaS marketing. The brand pairs domain-specific confidence (grounded in hard numbers: 85% recall rate, 8천만 건, 90% classification accuracy) with a direct, benefit-first Korean B2B register that speaks plainly to busy IP professionals who have no patience for buzzwords. The overarching personality is that of a trusted specialist colleague: precise, efficient, and respectful of the audience's intelligence.

---

## Voice Dimensions

### Formal <-----> Casual: 4/10

The copy leans moderately toward the casual end for a Korean B2B context — notably more approachable than a traditional law firm or enterprise software vendor, but never flippant. Tanalysis uses plain Korean (평어체 style) and addresses readers directly, which reads as collegial rather than corporate.

**Evidence:**
- "무수한 반복 작업, 줄여드릴게요!" — A casual contraction of willingness ("드릴게요") that sounds like a helpful colleague, not a press release.
- "복잡한 검색식은 NO, 문장으로 입력해도 AI가 정확하게 특허 검색" — The blunt "NO" mid-sentence is deliberately informal and punchy.
- "기준만 작성하면 끝" — Extremely clipped, conversational sentence. No subject, no formal verb ending.
- "경쟁사가 조용히 핵심 기술에 특허를 쌓고 있는 동안, 우리는 뒤늦게 알게 되는 경우가 생깁니다." — Shifts slightly more formal when addressing strategic risk, showing contextual register awareness.
- Pain point callouts in quotes ("리포트가 너무 두꺼워요", "경쟁사 특허 봐야 하는데…") mimic direct user speech, creating a conversational intimacy.

### Serious <-----> Playful: 3/10

Tanalysis is predominantly serious. The IP and patent domain demands seriousness — their users are professionals dealing with legal risk, competitive intelligence, and technical accuracy. There is no wordplay, no emoji, no self-deprecating humor. The one notable moment of levity is the occasional confident rhetorical punch ("복잡한 검색식은 NO"), but this is assertiveness, not playfulness.

**Evidence:**
- All testimonials are purely professional and outcome-focused: "불필요한 출원을 방지할 수 있었습니다," "효율성을 극대화할 수 있습니다."
- Pain points are presented with empathy, not humor: "결국 분기에 한 번, 그마저도 대충 훑게 됩니다."
- No exclamation marks overused; they appear only once per major CTA area ("줄여드릴게요!").
- No emoji observed anywhere in site copy.
- Stat callouts (85%, 90%, 5분, 30초) are delivered with data gravity, not celebration.

### Technical <-----> Simple: 4/10

Tanalysis occupies a deliberate middle zone: technical enough to signal credibility to patent professionals, but translated into benefit-language so IP managers and R&D leads (not just attorneys) can follow. The brand assumes domain literacy (청구항, CPC, FTO분석, 무효자료) but consistently pairs jargon with accessible framing.

**Evidence:**
- "특허청/심판원의 거절 및 무효 자료를 학습데이터로 사용한 유일한 특허 특화 모델" — Uses specialized terms (심판원, 무효자료) without apology, because the audience knows them.
- "복잡한 검색식 없이도 잘 이해하는 AI" — Reframes technical capability (semantic search) as everyday relief from complexity.
- Feature comparison table uses practitioner vocabulary (구성별, 선행문헌, 환각) yet is structured as a before/after benefit scan.
- "문장으로 입력해도 AI가 정확하게 특허 검색" — Strips technical architecture away; only the user experience outcome is stated.
- Stats section (검색 재현율 85%, 8천만 건, 30초 내) anchors authority in numbers a professional can verify and remember.

### Reserved <-----> Bold: 7/10

This is the brand's most distinctive dimension. Tanalysis is conspicuously bold for a Korean B2B product. It makes direct comparative claims, uses absolute language ("유일한," "압도적인," "다른 곳에 없는"), and names its evidence without hedging. This confidence is earned, not posturing — each claim is tethered to a specific data point or credential.

**Evidence:**
- "타사 대비 4배" — Direct competitive benchmarking, stated plainly.
- "다른 곳에 없는" used as a standalone H1 on two separate pages — a confident declaration of uniqueness.
- "'압도적인 분류 품질' 오직 타날리시스에서만" — Superlative claim delivered without qualification.
- "다 같은 AI가 아닙니다. 타날리시스는 다릅니다." — A direct competitive challenge in two sentences.
- "변리사와 AI 전문가가 처음부터 만들어, 정확도와 실무 적합도가 매우 높습니다." — Authority derived from maker credentials, stated with confidence.

## Brand Personality

**Primary Archetype: The Authority**
Tanalysis leads with credibility, expertise, and data. Every major claim is substantiated: a specific recall rate, a unique training data source, a user satisfaction score. The brand's ultimate trust signal is its origin story — "변리사와 AI 전문가가 처음부터 만들어" — which positions the product as built by the people who know what accuracy in this domain actually means.

**Secondary Archetype: The Innovator**
The brand's secondary register is forward-looking disruption. Phrases like "새로운 방식으로 검색해보세요," "다 같은 AI가 아닙니다," and "복잡한 검색식은 NO" frame Tanalysis as a break from the old way, not just an improvement on it. This innovator energy is disciplined, however — it always arrives with proof, not aspiration alone.

**Archetype Fit: Strong**
The Authority archetype is exceptionally well-matched to the target audience (변리사, in-house IP teams, R&D managers). These are professionals who evaluate tools on accuracy and domain credibility, not excitement. The innovator secondary layer gives the brand a forward momentum that prevents it from feeling stodgy.

## Vocabulary

### Words and Phrases Used Frequently

**Action words (favored verbs):**
- 줄이다 (reduce) — the dominant verb; almost every page promises reduction of burden
- 찾다 / 검색하다 (find/search)
- 분석하다 (analyze)
- 분류하다 (classify)
- 집중하다 (focus)
- 확보하다 (secure/obtain)
- 파악하다 (grasp/understand)
- 시작하다 (start) — always low-friction ("지금 가입하면 무료")

**Descriptive words (adjectives/adverbs):**
- 정확한 / 정확하게 (accurate/accurately) — the single most frequent qualifier
- 빠르게 / 빠른 (fast) — always paired with accuracy, never alone
- 쉬운 / 쉽고 (easy/easily)
- 압도적인 (overwhelming/dominant) — used for quality claims
- 유일한 (the only/unique) — used to mark true differentiation
- 실무에 딱 맞춤 (precisely fitted to practice)
- 특화 (specialized) — applied specifically to the AI model

**Value words (core to brand identity):**
- 실무 (practice/practical application) — legitimacy signal
- 전문가 (expert/specialist)
- 변리사 (patent attorney) — used as a trust anchor
- 심사관 (patent examiner) — the gold standard the AI emulates
- 효율 (efficiency) — the primary promised outcome
- 전략 (strategy) — the elevated goal users gain time for

**Industry-specific terms used without definition (audience assumed to know):**
- 청구항 (claims)
- 구성 (elements/components)
- 선행 문헌 (prior art)
- 무효 자료 (invalidation evidence)
- FTO 분석 (Freedom-to-Operate analysis)
- CPC
- 클레임차트 (claim chart)
- PCT

### Words and Phrases to Avoid

- Generic AI hype: "혁신적인," "게임체인저," "패러다임 전환" — too vague, too common, and inconsistent with the brand's evidence-first approach
- Passive hedging: "도움이 될 수 있습니다," "효과가 있을 수 있습니다" — Tanalysis makes direct claims backed by data; hedged language undermines authority
- Consumer-register words: "재미있는," "신나는," "즐거운" — out of character for a professional IP tool
- Competitor brand names in body copy (used in sample data only, not as general copy)
- "AI 솔루션" as a standalone descriptor — too generic; always specify what the AI does (검색, 분류, 분석)
- "최고의" (the best) without a specific, measurable claim attached — the brand earns superlatives through data, not assertion

### Signature Phrases and Linguistic Patterns

- **"다른 곳에 없는"** — deployed twice as a standalone H1; a declarative uniqueness claim that functions as a brand signifier
- **"변리사가 만들어 더 정확한"** — the footer tagline; condenses the core trust mechanism into one clause
- **"심사관 수준"** / **"심사관처럼"** — the professional benchmark Tanalysis measures itself against; recurring comparison
- **Pain-point quoting in speech marks** — "경쟁사 특허 봐야 하는데…", "리포트가 너무 두꺼워요" — acknowledging user language verbatim creates identification before the solution is presented
- **Benefit before feature** — "반복 작업은 줄이고, 중요한 전략 구상에 집중하세요" leads with the professional outcome, then the tool
- **Clipped declarative sentences** — "기준만 작성하면 끝." Short, complete, nothing to add. Used to signal ease.
- **Before/After framing** — the feature comparison table is the structural embodiment of the brand's contrast narrative: old way vs. Tanalysis way

## Writing Guidelines

### Do's

- **Lead with the outcome, follow with the mechanism.** "반복 작업은 줄이고, 중요한 전략 구상에 집중하세요" — the user's gain comes first; the AI is the means, not the headline.
- **Anchor every major claim to a specific number or credential.** "85%", "타사 대비 4배", "변리사와 AI 전문가가 처음부터" — data is the brand's primary trust currency.
- **Use the target user's own vocabulary.** When writing for patent attorneys, use 청구항, 구성, 무효자료 without explanation. When writing for R&D leads, explain briefly. Know which audience you're addressing.
- **Address the reader directly.** "오래 걸리시나요?", "찾는 내용까지 직행하는 검색" — second person and rhetorical questions create the practitioner-to-practitioner tone.
- **Use short, clipped sentences for emphasis.** "기준만 작성하면 끝." "타날리시스는 다릅니다." Brevity signals confidence.
- **Reflect user pain back before offering the solution.** Quote the user's frustration in their own words (in speech marks or as a named section header) before presenting the benefit.
- **Name the examiner benchmark explicitly.** "심사관 수준 이상의 높은 정확도" — the patent examiner is the universally recognized professional standard; use it.
- **Use active constructions for CTAs.** "지금 가입하면 무료", "소개자료 받기", "도입 문의" — short, verb-first, action-clear.
- **State uniqueness plainly when it is genuinely true.** "유일한 특허 특화 모델", "다른 곳에 없는" — if a claim is defensible, make it directly.
- **Write for scan, not reading.** IP professionals are time-constrained. Use tables, before/after comparisons, stat callouts, and bullet headers so value is extractable in 10 seconds.

### Don'ts

- **Don't use AI industry buzzwords without grounding.** Never write "혁신적인 AI" or "차세대 플랫폼" alone — always specify what makes it different (학습 데이터, 정확도, 속도).
- **Don't hedge claims that are supported by data.** "도움이 될 수 있습니다" is not the brand's voice. If the data supports it, state it directly.
- **Don't write longer than necessary.** This brand's rhythm is efficient. If a sentence can be cut in half without losing meaning, cut it.
- **Don't lead with the company's achievements before the user's problem.** Tanalysis's copy consistently opens on user pain or outcome, not corporate milestone.
- **Don't use generic CTA text.** "더 알아보기" alone is not enough. Pair it with a specific benefit or low-friction promise: "지금 가입하면 무료", "소개자료 받기".
- **Don't use humor or playfulness in technical or pain-point contexts.** The audience is dealing with professional risk; levity will undermine credibility.
- **Don't write in passive voice for key claims.** "높은 정확도가 달성됩니다" — no. "심사관 수준 이상의 높은 정확도" — yes.
- **Don't describe AI generically.** Always specify: what data was it trained on, what task does it perform, what makes it different from a general-purpose LLM. "상용 LLM의 환각 문제" is explicitly called out as what Tanalysis avoids.
- **Don't treat all audience segments identically.** Patent attorneys (변리사) care about recall rates and legal accuracy. R&D teams care about trend detection and speed. IP managers care about strategic insight. Adjust depth and vocabulary accordingly.
- **Don't omit the founder credential.** "변리사와 AI 전문가가 처음부터 만든" is the single most powerful trust signal the brand has. It should appear on any page where credibility needs to be established.

### Boilerplate (~120 words, English)

Tanalysis is a Korean B2B SaaS platform for AI-powered patent search, analysis, and classification, built by patent attorneys and AI engineers from the ground up. Its proprietary AI model is trained exclusively on patent examination and rejection data from the Korean Intellectual Property Office — 80 million patent documents across six jurisdictions (KR, US, JP, EP, CN, PCT). Tanalysis delivers a prior art recall rate of 85% in internal testing, 4x higher than comparable tools, with full-text search results returned in under 30 seconds. Used by patent law firms, in-house IP teams, and R&D organizations across Korea, Tanalysis replaces complex Boolean query workflows with natural-language element-by-element input, enabling IP professionals to focus on strategy rather than search mechanics. Tanalysis is headquartered in Gangnam, Seoul.

### Full Brand Story

**Current status:** Missing as a dedicated narrative. Present across the site in fragments (footer tagline, "Why Tanalysis?" navigation item, key stats), but no cohesive story page.

**Recommended narrative arc for an About or Brand Story page:**

The story opens with a practitioner truth: patent search is broken. Not because practitioners lack skill, but because the tools were never built by people who understood what accuracy in this domain actually costs when it fails — an incomplete invalidity search, an infringement risk undetected until litigation, a competitor's technology trajectory missed until it was too late.

Tanalysis was built to close that gap. Founded by a patent attorney and AI engineers, the company's defining choice was its training data: rather than licensing a general-purpose LLM and fine-tuning on patent abstracts (the standard approach), Tanalysis trained its model on the rejection and invalidation records of the Korean Intellectual Property Office — the actual decisions examiners make when they determine a prior art reference does or doesn't anticipate a claim. That choice is the source of the 85% recall rate. It is also why the tool produces results a practicing patent professional can rely on in a legal context, not just an exploratory one.

The brand's mission is a shift in where IP professionals spend their time: less on mechanical search and repetitive classification, more on the strategic judgments that only human expertise can make. Not AI replacing the attorney — AI doing the work the attorney shouldn't have to do manually.

**Differentiation Assessment:**

Tanalysis occupies a distinctive position in the voice landscape for three reasons:

1. **Bold specificity vs. institutional reserve.** KIPRIS and traditional Korean patent databases communicate in a government/institutional register: formal, passive, credential-listed but claim-free. Wipson is professional but cautious. Tanalysis breaks from this by making direct, data-anchored competitive claims ("타사 대비 4배") that are unusual in this category. This directness is a voice differentiator, not a risk.

2. **Practitioner empathy vs. feature cataloguing.** Most competing tools describe features. Tanalysis describes problems first — starting with "무수한 반복 작업" and "복잡한 검색식은 NO" — and then positions the tool as the relief. This pain-first structure is emotionally distinct in a category that tends to lead with technology.

3. **Innovator secondary layer within an authority primary.** PatSnap (the dominant global competitor) leads with innovation and data visualization. Tanalysis can differentiate by maintaining its practitioner-authority voice as the primary identity, while using innovator energy to signal that its AI approach is architecturally different from general-purpose AI tools. This is a unique territory: "the specialist, not the platform."

**Unoccupied Voice Territory:**
The market lacks a brand that speaks clearly to the IP strategy function at the C-suite and VP level — framing patent intelligence as competitive intelligence, not just legal process. Tanalysis's weekly report product already reaches toward this, but the brand voice on that page is still operationally focused. A more strategic, boardroom-register tone for competitive monitoring content would differentiate further.

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions

1. **Add the founding credential ("변리사와 AI 전문가가 처음부터 만들었습니다") to every primary product page**, not just the homepage and footer. This is the highest-trust signal the brand possesses and it is underused on the classification and weekly report pages.

2. **Sharpen the weekly report page's alarm register.** "이미 늦었을 수도 있어요" is the one phrase on the site that steps outside the brand's measured confidence voice. Replace with a consequence-without-panic framing: "경쟁사의 기술 방향을 분기에 한 번 확인하는 것으로는 부족합니다."

3. **Create a single 50-word boilerplate in Korean** for use in press releases, partner introductions, and event listings. The brand currently lacks one, leading to inconsistent self-description in external contexts.

4. **Add a 30-word "who built this and why" sentence to every page hero section** in secondary copy. The origin story is a conversion driver but currently lives only in the body of the homepage, not systematically across the funnel.

5. **Standardize the FAQ page opening tone.** Each FAQ article currently begins directly with the answer. Adding one sentence that acknowledges the user's question as reasonable ("이런 상황이 자주 있으시죠") before the technical answer would maintain the practitioner-to-practitioner warmth found in the main site copy.

### Voice Evolution Opportunities

1. **Develop a C-suite / IP strategy voice layer.** Tanalysis's current voice speaks most naturally to practitioners (변리사, IP managers). The weekly report product is a genuine strategic intelligence tool — but its copy positions it as a workflow convenience. To expand upmarket, develop a secondary voice register for VP-level and C-suite content that frames patent monitoring as competitive strategy, not operational efficiency.

2. **Write in the voice of a practitioner peer, not a vendor.** The brand is already close to this, but can go further. Consider introducing a blog or content series written by the founding patent attorney — in their own voice, addressing real patent strategy questions — that would embed the practitioner-authority identity in long-form content. This is the voice territory Ahrefs has built successfully in SEO: "we are the practitioners, building for practitioners."

3. **Develop an English-language voice variant.** As Tanalysis expands its database coverage (KR/US/JP/EP/CN/PCT) and potentially targets international IP professionals, an English voice guide will be needed. The core voice dimensions (practitioner authority, data-anchored boldness, benefit-first) translate well into English B2B copy conventions, but the sentence rhythm and formality calibration would differ from the Korean register.

### Consistency Improvements

1. **Create a CTA vocabulary guide.** Standardize the 6-8 CTAs used site-wide, specifying which CTA to use at which funnel stage. The current site has slight variations ("도입문의" vs. "도입 문의" vs. "타날리시스팀 문의") that suggest copy was written by multiple hands. Standardize these.

2. **Build a one-page voice reference card** for any new content contributors (freelancers, PR agencies, growth marketers). Include: the four voice dimension scores, 10 words to use, 10 words to avoid, 3 before/after copy rewrites, and the boilerplate. This document is the distillation of this full guide into a two-minute reference.

3. **Audit all microcopy** (button labels, form field placeholders, validation messages, loading states, empty states). These are currently not visible in the analyzed content but are high-frequency touchpoints. Ensure they carry the same direct, practitioner-respectful register as the marketing copy — particularly any error messages, which should always redirect immediately to a specific actionable next step rather than apologizing.
